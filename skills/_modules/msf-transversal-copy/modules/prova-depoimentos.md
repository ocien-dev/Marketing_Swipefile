## Job

Use this module to make a VSL, offer, ad, low-ticket product, or quiz choose
which proof should make a claim credible, where that proof belongs, and what
must be qualified so the final output does not overstate results.

## Retrieval Scope

Retrieve curated insights with `process-prova-depoimentos`. When the output is
a real process skill, combine this tag with the primary process tag.

## Playbook Claims

1. Treat first sales as only one kind of validation; stronger proof shows
   operational progress beyond the initial transaction.
   [insight:JF2oC44lBG8-v2-0005]
2. A low-ticket promise becomes more credible when the proof also shows that
   the offer is simple for the customer to understand or execute.
   [insight:TOW0sWhPaZw-v2-0007]
3. Accumulated audience trust or social proof can become a monetizable asset,
   but the process skill must still connect that proof to a concrete offer.
   [insight:aSFAve1klsc-v2-0007]
4. The value of an expert face depends on margin, backend, and proof fit; do
   not add an expert only because "authority" sounds persuasive.
   [insight:mCaFyZpXJdE-v2-0013]
5. The right expert combines proof, delivery, and communication; proof alone is
   not enough if the market cannot understand the message.
   [insight:zoChfFHnlOQ-v2-0004]
6. For VSLs, extract story, credentials, and transformation before writing the
   sale argument so proof supports belief instead of appearing as decoration.
   [insight:zoChfFHnlOQ-v2-0008]
7. Origin stories can act as proof when they show a real shift in commercial
   execution, not merely a background anecdote.
   [insight:JF2oC44lBG8-v2-0003]
8. The CTA should match the awareness level created by the creative and proof;
   proof that builds attention may still need a separate click reason.
   [insight:YcqJ_vrjf-g-v2-0003]
9. If the promise exceeds the perceived value of the deliverable, use proof to
   narrow or qualify the promise instead of amplifying the claim.
   [insight:mCaFyZpXJdE-v2-0011]

## Application Checklist

- Identify the exact claim that needs proof.
- Choose proof type: result, expert, demonstration, story, behavior, or
  operational milestone.
- Place the proof at the belief gap it resolves.
- Qualify high-risk claims and avoid implied guarantees.
- Cite the original `insight_id` for proof logic and evidence-backed claims.

## When Not To Use

- Do not use proof as decoration after the argument is already complete.
- Do not turn isolated sales, anecdotes, or authority cues into guaranteed
  outcomes.
- Do not rewrite evidence quotes or strip accents from verbatim quotes.
